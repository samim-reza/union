Union Policy Handbook

Complete reference of union governance, membership, contribution, loan, investment, and compliance rules.

SECTION A: FOUNDATION & IDENTITY

Policy 1: Name and Purpose

1.1 The union shall operate under the name "____________________".

1.2 Purpose of the union:

  • Monthly savings by members
  • Providing interest-free loans (Qard Hasan) to members
  • Halal investment of surplus funds for profit
  • Mutual financial support and discipline among members

1.3 Operating principles:

  • No riba (interest) in any transaction
  • No haram business or investment
  • No fraud, deception, or hidden transactions
  • Full transparency in all financial matters
  • Written record of every transaction
  • Fair treatment of all members

Policy 2: Fund Structure

2.1 The union's money is divided into 4 separate funds with separate records:

Fund Purpose Source
Savings/Loan FundMember savings and interest-free loansMonthly contributions (major portion)
Investment FundHalal business investments for profitMonthly contributions (portion) + reinvested profits
Reserve FundEmergency liquidity and protectionMonthly contributions (small portion) + charity penalties
Admin/Expense FundActual operational costsSmall monthly allocation

2.2 Monthly split:

  • ____% to Savings/Loan Fund (e.g., 60-70%)
  • ____% to Investment Fund (e.g., 20-25%)
  • ____% to Reserve Fund (e.g., 5-10%)
  • ____% to Admin/Expense Fund (e.g., 5%)

2.3 No fund shall be mixed with another without documented approval.

2.4 Loan Fund never generates profit.

2.5 Only Investment Fund may generate profit or bear loss.

SECTION B: MEMBERSHIP

Policy 3: Founding Members

  • Founding members are the ____ persons who sign on establishment date.
  • Required: legal name, contact, ID/passport copy, nominee details, policy acceptance signature.
  • Maintain a Member Register containing all details.

Policy 4: New Member Admission

  • Proposed by an existing member and introduced in a meeting.
  • Guest attendance for at least one meeting before joining.
  • Acceptance requires at least 75% vote of existing members.
  • Joining fee: ____ (non-refundable) + first monthly contribution.
  • Probation: ____ months; no loans, no major voting, profit share starts from contribution date.
  • Max members: ____ (e.g., 15-20).

Policy 5: Membership Obligations

  • Timely monthly contributions
  • Attend at least ____% meetings annually
  • Vote when present, repay loans on schedule
  • Follow policies and preserve confidentiality

Policy 6: Member Rights

  • Contribute, apply for loans, share profits, vote equally
  • View records, receive reports, propose agenda/rule changes
  • Nominate leadership and exit with settlement
SECTION C: CONTRIBUTIONS

Policy 7: Monthly Contribution Amount

  • Fixed minimum monthly amount: ____
  • Same minimum for all members
  • Annual review; 75% vote required for changes; 30-day notice before increase

Policy 8: Contribution Payment Rules

  • Due date: 1st of each month; grace up to ____th
  • Accepted: bank transfer, mobile money, cash with witness
  • Every payment recorded immediately with receipt/confirmation
  • No verbal promise counts as payment

Policy 9: Late Payment Policy

  • Late within month: warning, then temporary loan suspension based on repeat count
  • Monetary late penalties go to Reserve Fund or charity, never as profit
  • Treasurer contacts late members within 3 days after grace period

Policy 10: Missed Payment Policy

  • 1 missed month: catch up next month; loan eligibility suspended until caught up
  • 2 consecutive months: formal meeting, plan, written warning
  • 3 consecutive months: automatic suspension and committee decision on extension/exit

Policy 11: Hardship Exemption

  • Written hardship request; exemption up to ____ months by committee majority
  • No new loans during exemption; no penalties during approved window
  • One extension possible by 75% member vote

Policy 12: Extra / Voluntary Contributions

  • Extra can be designated as savings or investment capital
  • Undesignated extra defaults to additional savings
  • Extra amounts tracked separately
SECTION D: BANK ACCOUNT & FINANCIAL CONTROL

Policy 13: Union Bank Account

  • Dedicated account, not a personal account
  • At least 3 signatories, with 2-signature authorization for fund movement
  • No single-person control over funds
  • Monthly statement sharing mandatory
  • Bank interest (if any) removed and given to charity

Policy 14: Cash Handling Rules

  • Minimize cash use; digital preferred
  • Cash collection with witness + immediate receipt
  • Cash deposited within 48 hours
  • No personal use of union funds under any condition
SECTION E: LEADERSHIP & GOVERNANCE

Policy 15: Leadership Positions

  • President/Chairman, Secretary, Treasurer, Auditor
  • Investment Committee (2-3 members) for due diligence and recommendations

Policy 16: Election of Leaders

  • Simple majority elections at founding and every ____ year(s)
  • One role per member at a time
  • Max ____ consecutive terms per role (e.g., 2)
  • By-election within 30 days of vacancy

Policy 17: Removal of a Leader

  • Written motion by any 3 members + special meeting within 14 days
  • Leader has right to defend
  • Removal requires 75% vote of all members
SECTION F: MEETINGS & DECISION MAKING

Policy 18: Regular Meetings

  • Monthly fixed-day meeting with agenda shared at least 3 days earlier
  • Mandatory agenda includes attendance, reports, loans, investments, and issues
  • Minutes shared within 7 days

Policy 19: Annual General Meeting (AGM)

  • Annual review of finance, audit, leadership, policy amendments, and goals
  • AGM quorum: at least 75% of members

Policy 20: Emergency Meetings

  • Can be requested by any member for urgent issues
  • Called within 48 hours; quorum at least 2/3 members

Policy 21: Quorum and Voting Rules

  • Each member has one equal vote
  • Decision thresholds vary by category (routine, investment, admission, amendments, expulsion, dissolution)
  • Proxy and digital voting allowed with safeguards
  • Conflict-of-interest members must abstain
SECTION G: LOANS

Policy 22: Loan Eligibility

  • Active standing, minimum membership period, dues current, no unpaid existing loan
  • Probation members are not eligible

Policy 23: Loan Amount Limits

  • Use selected limit model (A/B/C/D) with clear cap
  • Total outstanding loans capped to preserve liquidity

Policy 24: Loan Application and Approval Process

  • Written application required with purpose and repayment plan
  • Committee review within 7 days
  • Higher amounts require full-member vote
  • Applicant cannot vote on own request

Policy 25: Loan Terms and Repayment

  • No interest/riba under any circumstance
  • Admin fee only for genuine costs
  • Equal monthly installments; early repayment allowed without penalty

Policy 26: Guarantors

  • Guarantor rules apply above chosen amount threshold
  • Guarantor liability terms must be signed and documented

Policy 27: Late Loan Repayment

  • Escalating reminders/warnings/suspension on repeated misses
  • No interest as late charge; any fixed penalty goes to reserve/charity

Policy 28: Loan Default

  • Default triggers a defined step-by-step recovery workflow
  • May include guarantor recovery, offset from savings, mediation, legal action, or write-off vote

Policy 29: Emergency Loans

  • Fast-track 24-48 hour review for verified emergencies
  • Separate emergency cap and extended repayment window allowed

Policy 30: Loan Priority System

  • Priority order: medical, death/funeral, survival, education, halal business, others
  • Deferred members remain first in queue for next disbursement

Policy 31: Prohibited Loan Purposes

  • No approval for haram or unethical purposes
  • Lying about purpose can make loan immediately due with disciplinary action
SECTION H: INVESTMENTS

Policy 32: Halal Investment Compliance

  • Only Shariah-compliant investments allowed
  • Prohibited sectors include alcohol, gambling, interest-based banking, and fraud-based activity
  • Doubtful cases require scholar guidance before approval

Policy 33: Investment Decision Process

  • Proposal, due diligence, discussion, vote, written contract, and monitoring are mandatory
  • Approval threshold: 75% of members present

Policy 34: Investment Limits & Risk

  • Caps for total investable amount and single investment exposure
  • Maintain minimum liquid reserve
  • No guaranteed returns

Policy 35: Investing in a Member's Business

  • Conflict-of-interest safeguards and independent review required
  • Member owner cannot vote on own proposal
  • Fraud/negligence may trigger personal liability

Policy 36: Islamic Investment Structures

  • Allowed structures: Mudarabah, Musharakah, Murabaha, Ijarah, Salam
  • Chosen structure must be declared in each contract
SECTION I: PROFIT & LOSS DISTRIBUTION

Policy 37: Profit Calculation Method

  • Profit calculated every ____ months
  • Profit source only Investment Fund
  • Net profit allocation split by voted percentages

Policy 38: Member Profit Share

  • Use Weighted Average Capital Balance formula for fairness
  • Members may cash out, reinvest, or split distribution

Policy 39: Loss Sharing

  • Loss shared proportionally by invested capital share
  • Recovery pursued for fraud/negligence cases

Policy 40: Zakat and Charity

  • Choose individual or collective Zakat handling model
  • Charity allocation from profit and non-permissible income cleanup
SECTION J: WITHDRAWALS

Policy 41: Partial Withdrawal

  • Written request + eligibility checks + yearly cap
  • Withdrawal impacts future loan limit and profit share

Policy 42: Investment Fund Withdrawal

  • Active invested money may not be immediately withdrawable
  • Uninvested cash portion may be withdrawn under reserve safeguards
SECTION K: MEMBER EXIT

Policy 43: Voluntary Exit

  • Written notice required (____ days)
  • Structured settlement formula applied
  • Liquid and locked portions paid according to timeline

Policy 44: Forced Removal / Expulsion

  • Defined misconduct categories and due process
  • Secret-ballot expulsion requires 75% of all members
  • Appeal rights preserved

Policy 45: Death of a Member

  • Nominee process governs settlement with required documents
  • Outstanding loan handling option chosen by union vote

Policy 46: Incapacity / Long-term Illness

  • Guardian/nominee may act on behalf of member with committee oversight
SECTION L: RECORD KEEPING & TRANSPARENCY

Policy 47: Required Records

  • Maintain member, contribution, loan, investment, cash, reconciliation, receipt, minutes, and correspondence records
  • Keep both digital and physical records with backups

Policy 48: Monthly Financial Report

  • Treasurer shares monthly report within ____ days of month-end
  • Includes balances, outstanding loans, updates, and issues

Policy 49: Audit

  • Internal audit every ____ months + annual external review recommended
  • Any member may inspect records within 48 hours of request
SECTION M: DISCIPLINE & MISCONDUCT

Policy 50: Disciplinary Framework

  • Misconduct classified as minor, serious, and gross
  • Penalties escalate from warning to suspension, expulsion, or legal action
  • Accused member retains right to fair hearing

Policy 51: Confidentiality

  • Member financial/privacy information is protected
  • Breach can lead to serious disciplinary action
SECTION N: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Policy 52: Dispute Resolution Process

  • Step 1: Direct discussion (7 days)
  • Step 2: Committee mediation (14 days)
  • Step 3: Full member meeting and vote (14 days)
  • Step 4: External mediation/arbitration (30 days)
  • Step 5: Legal action as last resort
SECTION O: WELFARE & SOCIAL FUND

Policy 53: Welfare Fund

  • Separate non-refundable social safety fund
  • Supports life-event grants (death, hospitalization, marriage, etc.)
  • Claims require reasonable documentation
SECTION P: AMENDMENTS

Policy 54: Amending Policies

  • Any member may propose in writing
  • Circulate at least 14 days before meeting
  • Requires 75% of all members (or higher for fundamental rules)
  • Maintain amendment version history with vote count and effective date
SECTION Q: DISSOLUTION

Policy 55: Dissolution of the Union

  • Dissolution via high-threshold vote or structural/legal triggers
  • Freeze operations, collect loans, liquidate assets, settle liabilities, final audit
  • Final distribution based on member capital accounts
SECTION R: MISCELLANEOUS

Policy 56: Communication

  • Primary chat channel + formal written notices for critical matters
  • All decisions must be documented in minutes/records

Policy 57: Legal Registration

  • Union should consider formal registration by 75% vote
  • Registration cost paid from Admin Fund

Policy 58: Inflation and Contribution Review

  • Annual review at AGM
  • Changes require 75% member vote with hardship protections

Policy 59: Use of Technology

  • Use digital tools for records, payments, and backup
  • Backups required at least monthly

Policy 60: Annual Review

  • Review policies, contribution amounts, allocations, leadership, investments, and yearly goals
